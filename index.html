Mission

To build sovereign, resilient compute infrastructure in rural North Dakota — making advanced AI, GPU compute, and technical education accessible to communities that have historically lacked access to it.

Arc Badlands Compute operates at the intersection of emerging technology and rural development. We are establishing GPU compute infrastructure, educational pipelines, and vocational certification programs in Mercer County — directly serving the Hazen, Beulah, and broader Bismarck-Mandan corridor while positioning North Dakota as a legitimate participant in the national AI infrastructure buildout. We are not yet grant-funded, not yet certified, and not yet formally incorporated as a nonprofit — but we are building with that intent at every layer of our architecture, governance, and program design.

⚡ GPU Compute Access
Providing on-demand GPU compute to researchers, educators, and startups across North Dakota — infrastructure typically found only in urban centers.
🎓 Vocational Education
Training programs in AI/ML operations, cloud infrastructure (GCP), and NVIDIA GPU management — building a credentialed technical workforce in rural ND.
🏔️ Rural Sovereignty
Designing for energy independence, sustainable operations, and resilient uptime — a community asset that does not depend on distant data center access.
🔬 R&D Infrastructure
Qualified Research Expenses (QRE) generating IRC §41 R&D credits and supporting academic, industry, and government computing needs across the region.

Federal Tax Provisions

IRC & OBBBA
Provisions We Target

One Big Beautiful Bill Act (2025) · TCJA · IRC R&D Framework

IRC §41
R&D Tax Credit Qualified Research Expenses (QRE) across our ML infrastructure, AI agent development, and GPU architecture work generate federal R&D credits — including payroll tax offset eligibility for small businesses.
IRC §174A
R&D Expensing (OBBBA Restored) The One Big Beautiful Bill Act restores domestic R&D expensing to immediate deduction (vs. 5-year amortization under TCJA). This significantly improves cash flow on our R&D-heavy operations.
IRC §168(k)
Bonus Depreciation 100% first-year bonus depreciation on qualified GPU and compute hardware. OBBBA extends this treatment, making hardware acquisition substantially more tax-efficient.
IRC §48
Investment Tax Credit (ITC) Energy property credit applicable to our renewable energy integration — solar, wind, and energy storage components of our data center design qualify for ITC treatment.
WOTC
Work Opportunity Tax Credit Hiring from targeted groups — including long-term unemployed and rural workforce populations in ND — generates per-employee federal credits, aligning our hiring mission with financial incentives.
QRE Exchange
Tax Credit Refundability Exchange For qualifying nonprofit and startup entities, excess R&D credits may be transferable or monetized. We are structuring operations to maximize this pathway as our 501(c)(3) status is confirmed.
Infrastructure Roadmap

GPU Stack
& Compute Architecture

Annual doubling cadence · VRAM proportionality maintained · GCP burst capacity

We are building a generational GPU stack anchored on NVIDIA architecture — from current V100 SXM2 capacity through Ampere, Hopper, and Blackwell, targeting annual hardware doubling while maintaining proportional VRAM across generations. Auxiliary P-series cards (P100, P40, P4) provide cost-effective VRAM expansion at 2× V100 density. Primary colocation target is DCN's Coleman Data Center in Bismarck. GCP scaling provides elastic burst capacity beyond on-premises limits.

Technical Foundation

Architecture
Philosophy

GoF · AOP · OOP · OOA · ML · LLM · Agent patterns · All disciplines, one system

Arc Badlands applies a rigorous, multi-paradigm architectural discipline to every layer of our systems — from GPU firmware to AI agent orchestration. We hold GoF (Gang of Four) design patterns as foundational, extend them through aspect-oriented, object-oriented, and ML-specific conventions, and maintain this discipline as a training objective for our vocational programs.

GoF / GoFour
  • Strategy — swappable algorithms
  • Template Method — campaign scaffolds
  • Observer — event fan-out
  • Facade — API gateway layer
  • Proxy — dry-run gate
  • Singleton — classifier instances
  • Composite — multi-notifier
  • Chain of Responsibility — sluice filters
  • Adapter — backend abstraction
  • Factory — vehicle config objects
AOP · OOP · OOA
  • AOP — cross-cutting concerns
  • Logging as aspect, not method
  • Auth & rate-limit as aspects
  • OOP — encapsulation first
  • Polymorphic campaign objects
  • Inheritance via ABCs (Python)
  • OOA — domain modeling
  • Repository pattern for data
  • Dependency injection
  • Interface segregation (SOLID)
ML / LLM Patterns
  • Map-Reduce for doc synthesis
  • RAG — retrieval-augmented gen
  • Two-tier vector architecture
  • Checkpoint-resume pipelines
  • Client pool + round-robin
  • Quota-aware retry backoff
  • Anti-hallucination guardrails
  • Sluice / classification filter
  • Prompt template injection
  • Multi-client load balancing
Agent / Systems
  • Cadence engine w/ signal gates
  • Inbound call triage workflow
  • Voice agent orchestration
  • Webhook fan-out architecture
  • SvelteKit PWA edge nodes
